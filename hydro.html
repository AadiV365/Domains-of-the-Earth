Hydrosphere

The hydrosphere is the combined mass of water found on, under, and above the surface of a planet, minor planet, or natural satellite.
There are 5 different oceans on the Earth.
The 5 different oceans are the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic Ocean.
Pacific Ocean - the largest and deepest of Earth's oceanic divisions.

Atlantic Ocean - the second-largest of the world's oceans, with an area of about 106,460,000 km².

Indian Ocean - the third-largest of the world's oceanic divisions, covering 70,560,000 km² of the water on Earth's surface.

Southern Ocean - also known as the Antarctic Ocean or the Austral Ocean, comprises the southernmost waters of the World Ocean.

Arctic Ocean - the smallest and shallowest of the world's five major oceans.
